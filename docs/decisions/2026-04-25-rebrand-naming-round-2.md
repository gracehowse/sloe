## 1. What round 1 ruled out, what round 2 hunts

Round 1 surfaced four PASS / workable-CAUTION single-word names: Repast (`.app`), Tureen (`.app` — pending TM call), Sorrel (`.kitchen`), Bramble (`.kitchen`). Only Repast clears at `.app`-with-prefix-`.com` cleanly; the rest accept TLD or TM compromises. Round 2 looks for **two-word compounds with a verified-available `.com`** that read at Linear/Notion tier and don't trip the Repast (funeral connotation), Sorrel (Sorel collision), Tureen (live food-class TM), or Bramble (UK food-shelf populated) lessons.

## 5. Top 3 PASS candidates

The picks below were chosen for inevitability of read, register-tone fit, and the cleanest collision profile.

### A. Daypantry — `.com` available

- **Plain meaning:** *Day* (the unit of one's eating day) + *pantry* (the cupboard where ingredients live, UK-coded).
- **What the compound evokes:** the small, calm store you reach into for the day's meals. It puts the user, not the algorithm, in charge of the next eating decision.
- **Welcome headline:** "Plan the day. Cook from the pantry."
- **Tagline:** "Recipes, planned. Macros, tracked. Calm pantry."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Daypantry** · Pro → **Daypantry Pro** · Premium → **Daypantry Plus**
- **Spoken:** "Daypantry dot com."
- **What would kill it in deeper TM clearance:** UK or EU food-class registration on "Day Pantry" (two words) not surfaced via web scan; possible UK Companies House filing for a small unrelated business under a similar mark. Likelihood: low — neither web nor TM databases turned up an exact-match collision, and "pantry" alone is too descriptive to monopolise.

### B. Plainkeep — `.com` available

- **Plain meaning:** *Plain* (honest, unornamented) + *keep* (the old-English noun for a fortified storehouse — also a verb meaning to preserve).
- **What the compound evokes:** an honest larder. The trade-old word "keep" pulls archaic-grown-up; "plain" pulls calm-confident. Reads like a Cotswold pub but for software.
- **Welcome headline:** "Plain food. Kept simple."
- **Tagline:** "Honest recipes. Honest numbers. Plainkeep."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Plainkeep** · Pro → **Plainkeep Pro** · Premium → **Plainkeep Reserve**
- **Spoken:** "Plainkeep dot com."
- **What would kill it in deeper TM clearance:** small UK Cotswold-style pub or B&B already trading as "The Plain Keep" — the archaic register is exactly the naming territory of UK hospitality. Web scan didn't surface one but a dedicated UK Companies House + IPO check is essential.

### C. Pantryside — `.com` available

- **Plain meaning:** *Pantry* + *-side* (alongside, in the manner of "fireside", "hearthside", "countryside").
- **What the compound evokes:** the same domestic anchor as Daypantry but with a softer, more atmospheric cadence. "By the pantry side." Closer to a B&B brand than a tech one — which is the whole point: warmer than Linear, colder than Hello Fresh.
- **Welcome headline:** "Cook from the pantry side."
- **Tagline:** "A calm place to plan, cook, and track."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Pantryside** · Pro → **Pantryside Pro** · Premium → **Pantryside Reserve**
- **Spoken:** "Pantryside dot com."
- **What would kill it in deeper TM clearance:** as above, a UK hospitality / cookbook trading name not indexed by Google. The "-side" suffix is generative enough that something may exist under a different domain. UK IPO Class 41/43 search needed.

## 6. Recommended #1 — Daypantry

**Pick: Daypantry (`.com` primary, no fallback needed).**

Daypantry wins because it is the only candidate of the eight PASS rows whose two halves are both already food-coded — no metaphor stretching needed to explain it to a first-time user. *Plainkeep* and *Pantryside* both work but require a beat of decoding ("what's a keep?" / "what's the pantry side?"); Daypantry reads on first sight as "the pantry of your day," which is exactly what a recipe + planner + tracker is. It also avoids the populated `-fork` category (the Gatherfork CAUTION) and the bare-Plum TM thicket (the Plumkeep CAUTION). Single-word `.com` at standard price, no TLD compromise, no hyphen, no archaic gloss to teach.

**What could kill Daypantry in deeper clearance:**
- A UK / EU Class 30 or Class 43 food-or-hospitality TM under "Day Pantry" not indexed by Google (lawyer search of UK IPO + EUIPO is non-negotiable).
- A pending recipe / nutrition / meal-planning iOS app in stealth using "Daypantry" before App Store listing — undetectable from public scan.
- "The Modern Pantry" (Anna Hansen, London) is an adjacent brand; "Daypantry" is distinct enough that it should not be a likelihood-of-confusion problem, but a UK lawyer should confirm.

## 7. Open questions for Grace

1. **Single-word vs compound.** Round 1's recommendation was Repast on `.app`; round 2's recommendation is Daypantry on `.com`. The two paths are mutually exclusive: pick one. The compound gets you a `.com` (billboard / radio / podcast advantage) but trades distinctiveness; the single word is more brandable in voice but loses on TLD.
2. **Tone of "keep" / "pantry" / archaic language.** Daypantry leans calm-and-domestic. If Grace's brand instinct is to read more like Linear / Cron (cooler, less domestic), then Plainfork or Quietplate may serve better — both PASS, both still on-tone, but harder-edged.
3. **TM-clearance budget allocation.** £1.5–3k full clearance per name. With 8 PASS rows and 2 CAUTION rows, sequence the clearance: lock #1 candidate, clear it, hold the runner-up as fallback. Don't pay for all eight at once.
4. **"Suppr Club" tier-name continuity.** The 2026-04-25 Suppr Club rename decision left an *eventual* community/membership tier called "Suppr Club" or equivalent. Daypantry Reserve, Daypantry Society, Daypantry Pantry — all workable but each tells a slightly different brand story. Decide in the same window as the wordmark.
