# Rebrand naming shortlist — 2026-04-25

**Status:** Open — recommendation pending Grace decision and lawyer-led TM clearance
**Owner:** Grace (final pick + registrar action), lawyer (clearance), executor (sweep)
**Supersedes-in-progress:** "Suppr" wordmark (kept by [2026-04-25 Suppr Club rename](./2026-04-25-domain-and-suppr-club-rename.md), but TM-collision risk to "Supper Club!" remains live — see `project_trademark_risk.md`)

> **This is not a TM clearance opinion.** Web checks against App Store, registrar APIs, Justia, and Google are coarse signal only. Any name from this shortlist needs a lawyer-led clearance search (US + UK + EU — minimum NICE classes 9, 42; possibly 41, 35, 30) before public launch or formal incorporation under the brand.

---

## 1. Brief recap

- Replace "Suppr" — phonetic collision with App Store competitor "Supper Club!" + non-registrable unhyphenated `.com` make the current wordmark a dead-end.
- Product is a recipe library + meal planner + macro tracker + barcode food log. iOS primary, web secondary. UK-built, global-ambition.
- Tone target: calm, confident, grown-up — Linear/Cron/Notion tier for software, but warmer because it's food. Counter-target: Noom/MacroFactor/MFP.
- Hard constraint: must look and sound right *and* not be already taken. Single common-word `.com` domains in this category are exhausted; the survivors live in compound coinages, archaic English, and botanical/agricultural fields with non-obvious twists.

## 4. Shortlist of PASS / CAUTION-promoted candidates

The four below survived all checks at PASS or workable-CAUTION (with mitigations stated).

### A. Repast — `.app` (recommended)

- **Positioning:** "A repast is a meal eaten at a fixed hour — a small, complete, uncoddled idea of food. Repast is the recipe + nutrition app for people who'd rather know what's in their food than be coached about it."
- **Welcome headline:** "Eat well, without overthinking it."
- **Tagline:** "Recipes, planned. Macros, tracked. Repast."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Repast** · Pro → **Repast Pro** · Premium → **Repast Plus**
- **Spoken:** "Repast dot app" (or `getrepast.com` / `repast.club` if Grace prefers `.com`-with-prefix).
- **Why it wins:** Two syllables, archaic-but-readable, no live food-app collision, no live food-class TM, .app domain available at standard price, plain-english pronunciation, transferable to UK/US/EU. Doesn't need a logo to be readable. Fits the calm-confident-grown-up bar.

### B. Tureen — `.app` (runner-up — pending TM risk-acceptance)

- **Positioning:** "A tureen is the lidded vessel that holds a shared meal. The app holds your week of meals, and your numbers, in the same calm container."
- **Welcome headline:** "Bring it all to the table."
- **Tagline:** "Recipes, plans, nutrition — one tureen."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Tureen** · Pro → **Tureen Pro** · Premium → **Tureen Reserve**
- **Spoken:** "Tureen dot app."
- **Why it could win:** More distinctive than Repast (lower brand-confusion ceiling). Same calm, food-anchored register. Same .app + .kitchen domain availability.
- **What kills it:** **Live USPTO TM #3252923 (TUREEN, Sensient Flavors LLC, food Class 30, autolyzed yeast extract).** Sensient is a B2B food-flavor-ingredient supplier; consumer-facing app likelihood-of-confusion is non-zero but arguable. **Demands a TM lawyer call before commitment** — if the lawyer says coexistence is plausible (different goods, different channels), it's the strongest pick on distinctiveness alone.

### C. Sorrel — `.kitchen`

- **Positioning:** "Sorrel is the bright, sour herb that lifts a dish. The app is the same idea: small, sharp signals that make your eating better without taking over your day."
- **Welcome headline:** "Cook what you want. Know what's in it."
- **Tagline:** "Sharp on macros, light on your day."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Sorrel** · Pro → **Sorrel Pro** · Premium → **Sorrel Garden**
- **Spoken:** "Sorrel dot kitchen."
- **Why it could win:** Botanical glow, premium feel, genuinely uncrowded. Two-syllable, easy to read, easy to spell.
- **What kills it:** `.kitchen` is a real TLD compromise. App Store doesn't care, but for a UK-first global launch, the .com that the user types after spotting your billboard matters. `Sorrel.com` is unobtainable; prefix-domains were not available either.

### D. Bramble — `.kitchen`

- **Positioning:** "Bramble is the tangle of good things you reach into and pick. The app is your library of recipes, your week's plan, and your numbers — already sorted."
- **Welcome headline:** "Pick what you'll cook this week."
- **Tagline:** "Recipes, planning, macros. Untangled."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Bramble** · Pro → **Bramble Pro** · Premium → **Bramble Hedgerow**
- **Spoken:** "Bramble dot kitchen."
- **Why it could win:** Strong botanical/foraged glow; UK-coded; feels grown-up.
- **What kills it:** Same `.kitchen` problem as Sorrel. Mild Bramble Berry (soap supplier) cross-traffic but truly unrelated category.

## 5. Recommended #1 — Repast

**Pick: Repast (`.app` primary, `getrepast.com` redirect, `repast.kitchen` defensive).**

**Why over Tureen:** Tureen is more distinctive, but the live USPTO food-class TM (Sensient Flavors) is a real legal-spend item before launch. Repast carries no equivalent live-mark risk surfaced in this scan, and gets to a clean `.app` at $15 with prefix-`.com` fallback. Distinctiveness is recoverable through wordmark + voice; clearance risk is not recoverable cheaply.

**What could kill Repast in deeper clearance:**
- Old-and-quietly-renewed UK or EU food-class TM not surfaced via Justia US — the `Repast` Java agent-modeling toolkit (academic) is unrelated, but a UK lawyer search of UK IPO + EUIPO is non-negotiable.
- A pending recipe / meal-planning app in stealth using "Repast" before App Store listing — undetectable from public scan.
- "Repast" reads slightly less warm than Tureen or Sorrel — a soft critique, not a blocker.

## Open questions Grace needs to answer

1. **`.app` vs `.com`-with-prefix.** `repast.app` is the cleanest read; `getrepast.com` keeps a `.com` for billboard / radio / podcast contexts. Pick one as canonical.
2. **TM clearance budget.** A full UK+EU+US clearance is ~£1.5–3k. Repast looks low-risk; Tureen requires it. Decide whether Tureen's higher distinctiveness justifies that spend before knowing if it's blocked.
3. **Tier-name continuity.** The 2026-04-25 domain decision left "Suppr Club" as an *eventual* community/membership tier. Does that survive the rename? Repast Club / Tureen Society / Sorrel Garden are workable equivalents — but we shouldn't promise it exists yet.
