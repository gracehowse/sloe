Round 1 surfaced single-word survivors at `.app` or `.kitchen` only. Round 2 surfaced 8 compound `.com` PASSes (top three: Daypantry, Plainkeep, Pantryside) but Grace hasn't committed. The hard rule this round: bare `.com` verified twice, App Store + UK food shelf + US TM + person-in-food-media all checked, no padding the PASS column.

## 5. Top PASS candidates

Only one. Plus the two best `.app`-route fallbacks (since round 1 already accepted `.app` for Repast / Tureen).

### A. Foltari — bare `.com` available

- **Plain meaning:** invented; reads natural-Italian (Italianate -ari ending; Italian "folto/folta" = thick, lush, dense, full); brand creates the meaning.
- **What the coinage evokes:** Italian-flavoured calm; fullness without excess; the lush interior of a well-stocked pantry. The seam between "folta" (thick) and "-ari" (Italianate noun ending) is invisible to English-speaking ears — reads like a real Italian word, but isn't.
- **Positioning sentence:** "Foltari is the recipe + nutrition app for people who want the calm of a full pantry without the noise of a coach."
- **Welcome headline:** "Eat well, without overthinking it."
- **Tagline:** "Recipes, planned. Macros, tracked. Foltari."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Foltari** · Pro → **Foltari Pro** · Premium → **Foltari Riserva** (Italian for "reserve" — keeps the world tight)
- **Spoken:** "Foltari dot com" — fol-TAR-ee (3 syllables; first-read pronunciation reliable for English speakers).
- **What could kill it:** Foltène (Italian dermocosmetic hair brand, Joy Group portfolio after Oct 2025 acquisition) is phonetically adjacent — different category and channel, low likelihood-of-confusion, but a UK / EU clearance lawyer needs to confirm against Foltène's actual EU classes. Also: Foltari is purely invented — no semantic glow on first hear. "What does Foltari mean?" requires a brand answer ("nothing — it's the calm of a full pantry"). Some users will bounce off invented words. This is the headline weakness.

### B. Hekla — `.app` (runner-up — `.com` blocked)

- **Plain meaning:** Hekla is an Icelandic stratovolcano (still active, occasionally lights up the south of Iceland); the word also means "short hooded cloak" in Old Norse, possibly because the summit is often clouded over.
- **What it evokes:** Quiet northern register; the cloak is a domestic / protective image, not a violent one; the volcano gives the brand visual energy without being aggressive (volcano-as-life-source, not destruction).
- **Positioning sentence:** "Hekla is a calm Northern recipe + nutrition app — domestic warmth wrapped around honest numbers."
- **Welcome headline:** "Eat well, without overthinking it."
- **Tagline:** "Recipes, plans, macros. Quietly."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Hekla** · Pro → **Hekla Pro** · Premium → **Hekla Reserve**
- **Spoken:** "Hekla dot app" — HEK-lah.
- **What could kill it:** `hekla.com` is registered (1996, Key-Systems registrar) — the bare `.com` is permanently out of reach without a private buy negotiation, and there's no signal it's for sale. `.app` works but loses the round-3 brief's bare-`.com` advantage. A `getheklap.com` / `tryheklak.com` / `heklafood.com` prefix path is technically possible (`heklafood.com` was confirmed available) but reads adventitious.

### C. Folta — `.app` (third — `.com` blocked)

- **Plain meaning:** Italian feminine adjective "thick / dense / lush" — the canonical phrase is *una folta nebbia* ("dense fog"), *una folta chioma* ("a thick head of hair"), *una folta vegetazione*.
- **What it evokes:** lushness, fullness, the woven texture of a forest or a pantry. Calmer than "Repast"; more sensory than "Daypantry"; reads natural-Italian to English speakers without feeling like a stretch.
- **Positioning sentence:** "Folta is the recipe + nutrition app for the lush interior of an honest kitchen."
- **Welcome headline:** "Eat well, without overthinking it."
- **Tagline:** "Lush food. Light tracking. Folta."
- **Tier names:** Free → **Folta** · Pro → **Folta Pro** · Premium → **Folta Riserva**
- **Spoken:** "Folta dot app" — FOL-tah.
- **What could kill it:** `folta.com` was registered to a Realtime Register holder in 1999, recently renewed in May 2025 — actively in someone's portfolio. `folta.app` is currently available but the same prefix-`.com` weakness as Hekla applies. Italian speakers will hear it correctly; English speakers will too on second exposure but may stumble on first-read (folter? folto?). Mid-strength for billboard/podcast contexts.

## 6. Recommended #1 — none of round 3 over round 2

Honestly: **none of the round-3 finalists is stronger than round 2's Daypantry, Plainkeep, or Quietplate.**

Foltari is technically a PASS, but the hand it offers is weak: a coinage with no English semantic glow, only a faint Italianate sound-feel, and a phonetic cousin (Foltène) in the cosmetics aisle that wants a clearance check. For Grace's brief — "calm, confident, grown-up, food/care register, plain-English readable" — Foltari fails the *plain-English* criterion. It needs the brand to teach the user the word. That's a marketing tax that rounds 1 (Repast: an English noun meaning "meal") and round 2 (Daypantry: two English food/domestic nouns concatenated) don't impose.

The two `.app`-route candidates (Hekla, Folta) each lose to round 1's Repast on the same grounds. Repast is shorter, English, food-coded, and already cleared a domain path in round 1.

**The honest recommendation: stop looking. Pick from rounds 1+2.**

If Grace wants to push the rebrand to a `.com` and accepts the compound trade-off, **Daypantry** (round 2 #1) remains the strongest candidate in the entire 90-name field. If she wants the single-word distinctiveness and accepts `.app`, **Repast** (round 1 #1) remains the strongest. Round 3 produced no candidate that beats either.

If Grace insists on a coinage answer for distinctiveness reasons (avoid descriptive-mark TM weakness), the order is: **Foltari (`.com`) > Hekla (`.app`) > Folta (`.app`)** — but the bar Grace should set for a coinage is "would I be willing to spend the marketing budget to teach this word?" If yes, Foltari works. If no, don't invent.

## 7. Comparison to rounds 1 + 2 winners

| Criterion | Repast (R1) | Daypantry (R2) | Foltari (R3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| TLD | `.app` (or `.com` with prefix) | `.com` bare | `.com` bare |
| First-read meaning | Yes — English noun "meal" | Yes — "the pantry of your day" | No — invented; needs brand teach |
| Distinctiveness (TM strength) | Mid — descriptive-of-the-product noun | Mid — descriptive-of-the-product compound | High — coined, inherently distinctive |
| Connotation risk | Yellow — funeral / archaic register | Green | Green |
| Phonetic collision | Green | Green | Yellow — Foltène (cosmetics) |
| App Store search distinctiveness | Mid — common English noun | High | Very high |
| Billboard / podcast spoken | Strong | Strong | Mid — needs spelling clarification on radio |
| Marketing tax to teach | Low | Very low | High |
| Domain-only legal cost | Low | Low | Low |
| TM-clearance projected cost | Low | Low | Low (no live mark surfaced) |
| Verdict | Strong PASS, slight coldness | Strong PASS, on-tone | Pass-on-paper, weak in practice |

**Would I actually pick Foltari over Daypantry?** No. Daypantry is more inevitable on first read, the same `.com`-bare grade, and the same low TM-clearance budget. Daypantry's only weakness vs Foltari is the same descriptive-mark weakness Repast has — but on a less common word ("pantry" is well-trodden but "Daypantry" specifically isn't).

**Would I pick Foltari over Quietplate?** Closer call. Quietplate is more on-tone (calm-grown-up-software register exact match) but the compound phonotactics are slightly worse than Daypantry. Foltari has higher TM-strength but loses on first-read. Still: Quietplate wins for me, narrowly.

**Would I pick Foltari over Plainkeep?** Yes, narrowly — Plainkeep needs the user to know what "keep" means as an old-trade noun, which is a similar marketing tax to Foltari's. Of the round-2 PASSes, Plainkeep is the most teach-required, and Foltari's higher distinctiveness compensates.

**Final ranking across 90 candidates:**

1. **Daypantry** (round 2) — `.com` bare, first-read clean, on-tone, low TM cost
2. **Repast** (round 1) — `.app` only, first-read clean, slight cold register
3. **Quietplate** (round 2) — `.com` bare, on-tone, slightly compound-y phonotactics
4. **Foltari** (round 3) — `.com` bare, coinage; only beats #5 if Grace specifically wants invented-word distinctiveness
5. **Plainkeep** (round 2) — `.com` bare, archaic register requires teach

## 8. Open questions for Grace

1. **Stop searching, or run round 4?** Round 3's PASS rate (1 of 32) is the floor signal that the bare-`.com` market for short, pronounceable, food-glow names is structurally exhausted. Further rounds will surface variants but won't beat Daypantry / Repast meaningfully. Recommendation: lock #1 from rounds 1+2 instead of running round 4.
2. **Coinage vs descriptive choice.** Foltari's higher TM-strength is a real long-term advantage (descriptive marks are harder to enforce). If Grace's planning horizon includes US TM filing inside year 1, the marketing tax of teaching "Foltari" may be worth the legal-strength gain. If TM filing is year-2 or later, descriptive picks (Daypantry, Repast) are fine and faster to launch.
3. **`.app` vs `.com`.** Repast on `.app` and Daypantry on `.com` are real alternatives, not a hierarchy. The `.com` advantage is mostly billboard/radio; `.app` is fine for App-Store-first acquisition. Decide before the rebrand PR.
4. **TM-clearance budget allocation.** Recommend clearing **one** name fully (UK + EU + US, NICE 9 + 42 + 41) rather than three half-cleared. Pick the candidate, then spend.
