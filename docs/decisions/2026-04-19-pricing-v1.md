**Date:** 2026-04-19
**Status:** Accepted
**Specialist:** monetisation-architect round-3

## Decision

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Saving |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | £0 | — | — |
| Base | £3.99/mo | £29.99/yr | 37% |
| Pro | £7.99/mo | £59.99/yr | 37% |

### USD reference (App Store Connect base currency)

| Tier | Monthly | Annual |
|---|---|---|
| Base | $4.99/mo | $37.99/yr |
| Pro | $9.99/mo | $74.99/yr |

Apple's UK tiering will resolve $4.99 → £3.99, $9.99 → £7.99, $37.99 → £29.99, $74.99 → £59.99 under current App Store brackets. Verify in App Store Connect after SKU creation — Apple controls the FX floor.

### Free trial

- **7-day free trial on Pro annual only.** No trial on Base. No trial on monthly (churn trap at this stage).
- Mobile paywall copy ("7 days free") is already correct.

### Launch discount

**None.** The published prices *are* the discount — calibrated below MyFitnessPal Premium (£7.99 mo match, but MFP has years of brand trust) and below MacroFactor's GBP equivalent. Explicit launch-window mechanics create either false urgency or dishonest lapses. Revisit after 60 days if conversion is lower than expected; introduce targeted codes per-channel then, not a sitewide banner.

### First price rise

Not coding on this now, but the intended gate is: **500 paying subscribers + App Store rating ≥ 4.3**. If either signal lands, review whether Pro should move from £7.99 → £9.99 and Base from £3.99 → £4.99 (matching MFP Premium+).

## Rationale

Suppr is pre-launch with no brand equity, no reviews, and an iOS app still in TestFlight. Price must remove hesitation, not price in confidence the product hasn't earned.

- **Base at £3.99/mo** sits in the budget band alongside Lose It!; £29.99/yr is competitive (below MFP, above Lose It!).
- **Pro at £7.99/mo** deliberately undercuts both MyFitnessPal Premium (same price, better brand) and MacroFactor's GBP equivalent, appropriate for a challenger with a tighter feature set.
- **37% annual discount** is mid-market. Industry band is 30–67% (aggressive apps go 75–79%); 37% is honest and defensible, not manufactured.
- **.99 endings** are the category convention. Every nutrition competitor uses them — round numbers at this price point would read as amateur or overconfident.

## Consequences

- Four Stripe price IDs needed: `STRIPE_PRICE_BASE_MONTHLY`, `STRIPE_PRICE_BASE_ANNUAL`, `STRIPE_PRICE_PRO_MONTHLY`, `STRIPE_PRICE_PRO_ANNUAL`. Documented in `docs/environment.md`.
- Four App Store Connect SKUs needed: `base_monthly`, `base_annual`, `pro_monthly`, `pro_annual` under bundle `com.supprclub.supprapp`.
- RC offering (one "current" offering) contains four packages, one per SKU.
- `PRICING_TIERS` in `src/lib/landing/content.ts` extended with `annualPrice`, `annualPeriod`, `annualSavings` — pinned by `tests/unit/landingParity.test.tsx` (GBP-only, paid tiers must carry annual fields).
- Mobile paywall ([apps/mobile/app/paywall.tsx](apps/mobile/app/paywall.tsx)) prefers the annual Pro package so the 7-day trial framing matches, falls back to monthly when annual isn't provisioned.

## Deferred

- **Android.** No Android build yet; no store products needed until one exists.
- **Promo-code mechanics.** Stripe `allow_promotion_codes: true` is still set. No iOS promo flow — if needed later, use Apple's native offer codes feature.
- **Region-aware pricing.** GBP is hard-coded as the fallback in `PRICING_TIERS`; Apple's `priceString` handles real-world locale on iOS. Tracked as a broader piece in `project_region_aware_pricing` memory — different problem, different session.

## Resolved follow-ups (same-day round 2)

- **Base on mobile iOS — RESOLVED.** Full redesign of `apps/mobile/app/paywall.tsx` shipped 2026-04-19 per `ui-product-designer` spec (Option C). Two tier cards (Pro + Base), monthly/annual toggle, per-card CTAs, trial timeline only when Pro annual is selected, single composite disclosure, early-redirect if user already Pro-entitled, partial-provisioning edge cases. Feature lists are inlined (mirror of `PRICING_TIERS`) because `src/lib/landing/content.ts` imports `@/…` aliases that don't resolve in mobile's tsconfig graph. Leaf-extraction is a future cleanup, not scope here.
- **Consumer VAT posture — FLAG-GATED (round 6, 2026-04-19).** Per `docs/decisions/2026-04-19-consumer-vat-posture-uk-eu.md`, the web disclosure and Stripe Checkout tax wiring both read `STRIPE_TAX_ENABLED`. Default `false` ships the pre-round-4 `"Price excludes any applicable taxes."` line AND omits `automatic_tax` from Checkout — truthful while the Stripe dashboard flip is still pending. Flipping `STRIPE_TAX_ENABLED=true` in prod activates both the `"Price includes any applicable VAT."` copy and `automatic_tax: { enabled: true }` + `billing_address_collection: "auto"` on Checkout in lockstep. Mobile disclosure is flag-independent — Apple handles VAT-inclusive display natively per storefront.
