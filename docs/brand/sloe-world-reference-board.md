# Sloe — World / Interior Reference Board

> **What this is:** a running board of real-world *interior + lifestyle* references that feed the
> **environment, prop kit, light language, and brand ethos** behind the locked Sloe test kitchen.
> It is **input, not spec** — it informs (and never overrides) the authoritative files:
> [`sloe-food-photo-house-style.md`](./sloe-food-photo-house-style.md),
> [`sloe-image-prompt-template.md`](./sloe-image-prompt-template.md), and the palette in
> [`sloe/sloe-brand.md`](./sloe/sloe-brand.md). Material absorption into those specs is a
> `brand-manager` decision (house-style §11 change control). This board just keeps the references —
> and the *specific* cues worth stealing — from being stranded in chat.
>
> **Scope note:** these are *home-interior* references. Their value is the **world** the brand lives
> in — set, surfaces, metal, light, props, mood, voice — **not** food plating. Plating rules
> (camera/angle/crop) stay governed by the house-style §1–§6 and are untouched by this board.

## Entry 1 — Jade Wilson (@jadewilson.f) kitchen + pantry + dining reels (Grace, 2026-06-07)

Grace tag: *"more sloe kitchen inspo."* Five frames: a butler's pantry, a cream island kitchen with
rush counter stools, and a light-flooded dining room with a black linear chandelier.

### Verdict — convergence, not drift

This is **the locked Sloe test kitchen rendered in a real home**, not a new aesthetic. The
house-style already specifies a marble galley with cream Shaker cabinets, brass hardware, copper, a
side window, pale wood floor, and soft warm directional daylight — and every frame here *is* that
room. So this board entry's job is to extract the **three refinements** these reels add beyond what
the spec currently says, plus one voice gem.

### Already covered by the locked spec (confirms we're on-target)
- Cream Shaker cabinetry · honed white marble/quartz counters · pale white-oak floor.
- Brass as the single warm metal · warm directional side-window daylight, bright-and-airy, soft shadows.
- Linen (roman shades + drapes) · ceramic/stoneware · glass jars · restraint + negative space.

### NEW cues these reels add (candidates for brand-manager to absorb)

1. **Unlacquered / aged ("living-finish") brass — not bright polished brass.** The hardware here is
   warm and slightly patinated (the long fridge bar-pull especially). Recommend the spec say
   *"unlacquered/aged warm brass, soft sheen, never bright-chrome-shiny"* so generated kitchens don't
   render cold mirror-gold. Maps cleanly to the brand's warm **Honey `#D6A24A`** / **Clay `#C8794E`**
   accent register, never to cool plum/Frost.

2. **One restrained matte-black graphic accent is allowed.** The dining room's matte-black linear
   chandelier (and black window mullions) is the single dark note against an all-cream field — it
   reads *considered*, not loud. This is the home-decor analogue of using **Ink `#221B26`** as a
   sparing graphic anchor on the Oat ground. Recommend: *"a single matte-black architectural accent
   (lighting/mullion) permitted as a graphic anchor; never a black surface or backdrop."*

3. **Prop-kit extensions for Tier-1 lifestyle (Template D) frames** — rush/rattan stools with linen
   cushions + tie-bows · white linen empire/cone pendant shades on brass chains · a gold-framed
   still-life oil painting · ceramic urns with fresh stems (tulips/hydrangea) · a worn wooden dough
   bowl · glass storage canisters · beadboard/shiplap ceiling + classic millwork. These enrich the
   *lifestyle* set (app-resting-in-a-warm-kitchen), **not** the per-dish plating prop list (§4 stays
   "2–4 functional props, garnish = the dish's own ingredients").

### Voice gem — the caption *is* the thesis
*"things in my home I'll never regret investing in."* This is the **slow** brand thesis in someone
else's words: patience, permanence, heirloom-quality, no-regret slow choices — the homophone made
felt, never stated. Worth a line in the marketing/landing voice swipe file (route to `brand-manager`
+ `copy-reviewer`). It rhymes with the locked taglines ("Cook what you love. Hit your goals anyway."
/ "The calm way to eat well.") and with the *Sloe = slow, things that last* name story.

### Entry 1b — profile grid + "the ONE thing in my remodel" close-ups (second batch, same session)

A second pull from the same creator (profile grid + close-up reel frames) sharpens the picture and
adds one structural decision plus a much richer voice set.

**Confirms (now read as signatures, not one-offs):**
- **Copper pots on a brass wall-rail beside the range** — appears in nearly every frame. The locked
  spec already says "copper pots on the range"; this is strong corroboration. Keep it.
- **Aged-brass everywhere**: bridge/gooseneck faucet + brass side-spray + brass pot-filler, cup-pulls
  + knobs. Reinforces the *unlacquered/aged* refinement in Entry 1.
- Warm-wood furniture layer (dark sideboards/hutches) + ceramic urns with dried hydrangea + woven
  seagrass trays + speckled ceramic bowls/colanders + antique brass pepper mill + glass canisters.

**NEW structural cue — the two-tone kitchen (decision-worthy):**
- A **dark stained-oak island** (white quartz top, brass hardware, white apron-front/fireclay sink)
  set against the **cream Shaker perimeter**. This is a deliberate dark **mid-tone contrast anchor** —
  the locked Sloe test kitchen is currently *all* cream + marble + pale wood, so this is a genuine
  fork: stay all-cream, or adopt the cream-perimeter + dark-wood-island contrast. The dark wood maps
  to Sloe's warm-wood accents (and tonally toward **Ink**), so it's on-palette — but it changes the
  *room*, which is a Tier-1 brand asset. **Recommend:** offer it as an approved *variant* of the
  locked kitchen (same light/brass/marble/cream DNA, island free to go dark oak) rather than replacing
  the all-cream room — gives marketing-frame variety without splitting the recognisable set.

**NEW set/architecture cues:**
- **Plaster arched range niche + plaster range hood** (soft, hand-troweled, no hard edges).
- **White apron-front / fireclay farmhouse sink**.
- **Art genre, specified:** moody pastoral *landscape* oils + still-life *floral* oils, in thin gold
  frames — leaned against the backsplash or hung over sideboards.

**Voice gems (slow-living vernacular — route to `brand-manager` + `copy-reviewer` for the swipe file):**
These are the *slow* thesis in a lifestyle creator's own words — unusually on-brand:
- *"a slow Sunday morning"* — almost verbatim the brand; strongest single line.
- *"the older I get the more I romanticize staying home"*
- *"making my home feel softer one little detail at a time"*
- *"created the home I kept saving on Pinterest"*
- *"things in my home I'll never regret investing in"* (Entry 1)
- The throughline — *home as unhurried sanctuary, softness, permanence, slowness* — is the Sloe
  positioning rendered in a different category. Mine for landing/onboarding/social copy tone, never
  for in-app chrome.

**Suggestion — pin @jadewilson.f as a named interior reference.** Two consecutive same-creator drops
is a strong signal. Just as the food-photo style names `@_foodstories_` / `@thelittleplantation` for
*dishes*, recommend naming **@jadewilson.f** as the pinned *interior/world* reference for the Sloe
kitchen set + lifestyle (Template D) frames — so the "world" has a fixed touchstone the way the food
does.
