## Why Sloe
- **The blackthorn berry** — earthy, garden, homely; ties to the warm-coaching direction.
- **"Say it slow"** (/sləʊ/) — calm, unhurried pace; the brand's whole posture, felt not stated.
- **Carries the positioning** — "Cook what you love. Hit your goals anyway."
- **Frost bloom = ownable accent** — the berry's frosted bloom (#C9C2D6) is a colour no competitor
  in the recipe/nutrition lane holds.
- Domains being grabbed: **eatsloe.com**, **sloe.life**. Developer name: "Sloe Labs".

## Reconciliation with the locked direction (important)
Sloe is **additive**, not a clash:
- The **in-product functional palette stays**: Clay = warm CTA/Pro/encouragement (≈ our terracotta),
  Sage = success/under-budget, Amber = macros/sodium over (NOT ring), Honey = best-day star,
  Destructive = ring-over-budget ONLY. The **locked calorie-ring colour rule is preserved**.
- **New brand-identity layer on top**: Sloe plum (primary wordmark/ink/mark), Damson (active/lifted),
  Frost (ownable light accent), brand gradient (Damson→Sloe→Frost) for marketing / paywall / empty ring.
- **Ground — RESOLVED (Grace 2026-06-02): split by surface.**
  - **APP UI ground = pure white `#FFFFFF`** — clean, chic, modern. The white-base call HOLDS in-product.
  - **MARKETING ground = Oat `#FBF8F3`** — warm canvas for App Store screenshots, landing, paywall promo,
    social. Painterly produce/botanical illustrations bleed in to frame the device (the Julienne
    App-Store treatment: onion/lemon/celery on oat around a white-UI phone + serif headline).
  - Bonus synergy: those marketing botanicals are the SAME painterly ingredient illustration system
    we're keeping (eggs/blueberries style) — reuse, don't recommission.
