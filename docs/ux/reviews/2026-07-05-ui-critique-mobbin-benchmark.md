## Verdict

Three product-level failures dominate everything else:

1. **No food in the product.** Across all six primary iOS captures there is not one photograph of food. The Recipes hero is a pure-white rectangle; "What to eat next" is an olive gradient with a fork glyph; the paywall hero renders as blank cream despite a real bundled photo. The first-week experience is the product for every new user and App Store reviewer in the launch window — and it is the least-designed thing in the app.
3. **The numbers don't agree with each other.** 929 vs 1,402 vs 1,941 for one digest week; "Fridays ran higher than Thursdays" citing unlogged days; GOAL/RATE em-dashes above a computed projection; "0 kcal maintenance" above "MAINTENANCE 2,117"; "Yesterday" heading over "Today's Meals". For an Oura/MacroFactor audience, irreconcilable arithmetic reads as fabricated data. Per the project's own rule, the nutrition-trust cluster is never-defer.

## Whole-product judgment (identity pass)

Sloe currently has a typographic identity, not a product identity. The serif-plum voice is real and genuinely ownable — the eyebrow-plus-serif-headline grammar (TODAY/Saturday, YOUR TRENDS/Progress, COOK/Your kitchen, SLOE PRO/Cook what you love) survives every one of the ten captures on both platforms, the italic emphasis on the paywall headline is a confident brand moment, and dark-mode Today shows the palette can carry mood. That spine is worth protecting; nobody else in the tracker category has it. But identity runs on four channels — type, colour, materiality, imagery — and Sloe is shipping on one. The light palette photographs cool lavender-grey, not the warm cream the direction promises (measurable across today-top, log-sheet, recipes-top, paywall); depth is a single soft shadow on flat pale cards, with the one attempt at texture (the chevron watermark) appearing as a rogue one-off inside two placeholder voids; and there is not a single photograph of food anywhere on the primary platform across all six iOS captures — the only appetite moment in the entire set is the web recipe-detail salad. A food product whose iOS surface contains zero food is not warm-editorial; it is a beautifully typeset wireframe.

The deeper failure is that the product's dominant visual material, as actually experienced, is undesigned emptiness. Every hero on every surface collapses at low data: the Today ring is a ghost tick-circle indistinguishable from a spinner, the Recipes flagship module is a blank white rectangle, the paywall hero is a cream void, Plan is a guilt-wall of dashed boxes and three flavours of zero, Progress leads with a spinner-lookalike arc and a chartless weight card. The app recites its own absence — GOAL 0/EATEN 0/BONUS 0, "0 saves", "0 of 7 days land", AVAILABLE 0/EARNED 0/USED 0 — and where it does speak, the numbers contradict each other (929 vs 1,402 vs 1,941 for one week; "0 kcal maintenance" above "MAINTENANCE 2,117"; "Yesterday" over "Today's Meals"). Emotional temperature against the bar: Oura is calm assurance, Lifesum is warmth; Sloe as captured is cool, administrative, and occasionally defensive ("No paywall. No asterisk.", allergen disclaimers outranking ingredients). Verdict: a set of screens sharing a masthead, roughly two tiers below the stated bar — but the gap is state-design and craft discipline, not concept. The concept is right and the bones are visibly good; nothing here needs a redesign, it needs finishing.

### Web ↔ iOS parity verdict

Web and iOS do not read as one product with platform adaptations; they read as two teams shipping parallel products from a shared wordmark and palette. The splits go three levels deep. First, they disagree on data: iOS greets the user as 'gracehowse+test' while web says 'Data-Rich Tester (persona)'; iOS shows GOAL/RATE as em-dashes where web shows 66 kg and −0.2 kg/wk; the same digest week averages 929 on web and 1,402 on iOS; iOS says 0/3 days logged where web says 1/3. Second, they disagree on anatomy: the log surface is a full sheet with meal pills, quick actions, thumbnails, and a running tally on iOS but a stub dialog with none of those on web; recipe detail is a full-screen route on iOS but an embedded panel under three layers of kitchen chrome on mobile-web; the Week Digest is a rich module on iOS and plain paragraphs on web; Plan's core action is a filled Generate hero on web and an unlabeled sparkle icon on iOS; the household switcher exists on web Plan and not iOS Plan; Settings is a sub-nav SaaS page on web and a bare scroll on iOS. Third, they disagree on vocabulary: Library vs Cookbook, 'Add to today' vs 'Search Foods', All/Recent/My foods/Branded/Generic vs All/Custom/Branded/Generic.

Two things make this worse than ordinary drift. The direction of failure is bidirectional despite iOS being the declared primary surface — web leads on Plan's CTA, goal data, weekly visualisation, and labeled create/import buttons, while iOS leads on log-sheet anatomy, macro rendering, and hero legibility treatment, so neither platform is the canonical reference and every new surface forks again. And mobile-web — the exact surface an MFP refugee lands on from an Instagram link — is the worst of the three renditions everywhere it appears: colliding chrome, truncated names, missing macro numbers, modals that don't own the viewport. The fix is not per-screen patching: pick the canonical anatomy per surface (per the peer-review model, iOS leads, web inherits), write it down as a one-page anatomy spec per surface, move all derived data into shared selectors, and treat any structural divergence without a documented platform reason as a build failure — the same discipline the token ratchets already apply to colour and spacing, applied to structure.

### Systemic moves (ranked by leverage)

#### 1. Design the first-week state as a system — one empty/sparse-state grammar for every hero  `leverage: highest`

**Evidence:** Every surface's emotional centre fails at low data, each in its own ad-hoc way: Today's ring at 0 is near-invisible grey ticks reading as a stuck spinner (today-top.png, dark-today.png worse); Recipes' Tonight's Pick renders a blank white rectangle filling ~40% of the viewport (recipes-top.png); Progress leads with an unlabeled partial arc that reads as a hung loader plus a Weight card whose Trend/Scale toggle switches an invisible chart (progress-top.png, progress-desktop.png); Plan is four dashed boxes plus '0 of 7 days land' under an 'On track' verdict; the paywall's degraded branch ships price-less (paywall.png); web ships a Streak-freezes card of three giant zeros. Eight surfaces, eight different improvisations, zero designed.

**Fix:** Write one empty-state contract and apply it everywhere: (1) heroes never render at zero visual weight — empty tracks/washes get a warm tint one elevation step above the background; (2) every empty hero carries exactly one invitation action inside it (filled 'Log breakfast' in the ring, 'Generate this week' on Plan, 'Log weight' inside the chart frame); (3) numbers are suppressed until earned — no zero triads, no averages from one data point, no toggles on invisible charts; (4) degraded/fallback branches (paywall offerings-empty, missing hero image) are designed states with the same care as the happy path. Ship it as a checklist gate: no surface merges without its empty, sparse, and degraded states captured. This is the highest-leverage move because the first-week experience IS the product for every new user, the App Store reviewer, and the launch window.

#### 2. One number pipeline — shared derivation, reconciliation-tested, both platforms  `leverage: highest`

**Evidence:** The product's numbers disagree with each other across and within surfaces: the same digest week renders 929 avg (web), 1,402 AVG CAL (iOS), while its only logged day is 1,941; 'Fridays ran higher than Thursdays' cites two days the digest says were never logged; iOS shows GOAL/RATE as em-dashes two cards above a projection that computes a pace, while web shows GOAL 66 kg, RATE −0.2 kg/wk for the same account (progress-top.png vs progress-desktop.png); Today's Activity card says '0 kcal maintenance' directly above 'MAINTENANCE 2,117'; desktop heading says 'Yesterday' while every section below says 'Today' (today-desktop.png); Plan says 'On track' over a completely empty week.

**Fix:** Move every derived stat — averages, adherence, maintenance, goal/rate/projection, viewed-date labels — into shared selectors in @suppr/shared that both platforms consume, with reconciliation tests (avg-on-logged-days must equal the mean of rendered day values; comparisons suppressed unless both operands exist; verdict copy gated on data existing; all date-scoped labels bound to the viewed date). Then adopt the auditable-math discipline already present in the maintenance card (929 − 2,117 = −1,188) everywhere a claim is made. For an Oura/MacroFactor audience, irreconcilable arithmetic reads as fabricated data — this is the brand-premium fix wearing an engineering costume, and per the project's own rule the nutrition-trust cluster is never-defer.

#### 3. Put food in the product — an imagery system that cannot fail to white  `leverage: highest`

**Evidence:** Across six iOS captures there is zero food imagery: Recipes' hero is pure 255-white while identical placeholders render below it (recipes-top.png — the placeholder pipeline itself fails in the flagship code path); Today's 'What to eat next' is a muddy olive gradient with a fork glyph; the paywall hero region samples flat rgb(247,246,250) despite a real bundled photo (paywall.png); recipe-detail iOS opens on ~280pt of grey void; even the placeholder system is non-deterministic (same tofu bowl gets dash-grid on desktop, dot-grid on iOS). The only appetising pixel in ten captures is web's Greek-salad hero (recipe-detail-desktop.png).

**Fix:** Three layers, in order: (1) fix the hero render paths so the existing placeholder/asset layer cannot resolve to blank white — every image container gets a tinted background beneath it as a structural guarantee; (2) make placeholder identity deterministic per recipe ID (frozen tint + pattern + larger duotone food illustration keyed to category) so the same recipe looks identical everywhere; (3) ship the AI image-generation project into the hero slots — Tonight's Pick, What-to-eat-next, recipe detail, paywall — because the warm-editorial direction is literally impossible without imagery. Rule going forward: no full-bleed module ships without a designed no-image state.

#### 4. One component grammar for the five primitives every surface repeats  `leverage: high`

**Evidence:** The same five elements render differently on nearly every sighting: selected pills — lilac-fill Breakfast vs solid-plum All on ONE iOS screen (log-sheet.png), web using iOS's meal-pill style for filters; segmented controls — D/W/M/6M/Y is a pill track while Trend/Scale on the very next card is bare text (progress-top.png), web renders the same toggle as a pill; add-affordances — four treatments in one log surface (filled circle + orange glyph, bare plum +, star + chevron, chevron only); avatar initials — 'D(' with a literal parenthesis on both iOS and web (progress-top.png, today-desktop.png sidebar 'D'); horizontal scrollers — content clipped flush mid-glyph with no fade or peek in at least six sightings (Snacks on log-sheet, Dessert on recipes-top, Mia chip on mobile-web, quick-add 'Chicke..' on today-desktop, 'Ge…' filter, Plan meal row).

**Fix:** Build the five as single shared components with one spec each — SelectedPill (lilac-fill; solid plum reserved for primary CTAs), SegmentedControl (pill track), AddControl (one circular ≥36pt treatment used by favourites, history, matches, plan slots), Avatar (shared initials util: strip non-letters, first letters of first two words, unit-tested), HScroller (built-in trailing inset + edge fade + half-item peek) — then sweep every surface to consume them in one pass per primitive. This is days of work, not weeks, and it removes the loudest 'assembled, not designed' tells in the entire product simultaneously.

#### 5. A bottom-chrome contract: floating bars reserve space, sheets own the viewport  `leverage: high`

**Evidence:** One defect class, six sightings across four surfaces: Recipes card titles render interleaved with the floating tab bar's labels (recipes-top.png, y≈670–690pt); Settings' tab bar falls into the scroll flow inside the membership cards; mobile-web's log modal has the tab bar and FAB floating ON TOP of the open sheet with a clipped '…before saving.' string behind the FAB; the paywall comparison table is decapitated at its header row by the sticky footer (paywall.png — FREE/PRO labels visible, all six data rows hidden); recipe-detail mobile-web stacks two colliding footers with the Log pill sliced to an 8pt arc; iOS recipe detail scroll ploughs into the status bar with no scrim and content ghosts legibly through the flat-translucent sticky footer.

**Fix:** Codify three rules as shared layout primitives, not per-screen fixes: (1) every scroll surface under a floating bar gets bottom inset = bar height + one spacing token, plus a cream gradient scrim behind the bar so under-content fades instead of interleaving; (2) an open sheet/modal owns the viewport — tab bar and FAB are suppressed or scrimmed behind it, with safe-area padding for its last row (iOS's native sheet already proves this; mobile-web must inherit it); (3) sticky footers use real blur material and their scroll containers get matching contentInset so nothing ships decapitated. Wire the recipes-top and paywall framings into the visual-QA capture checklist as regression pins.

#### 6. Warm the shell — shift the palette's temperature and give depth a second step  `leverage: high`

**Evidence:** The stated direction is warm cream + dark plum ink; the captures photograph cool lavender-grey across every iOS light surface (today-top, log-sheet, recipes-top, progress-top, paywall all sample in the #f5f4f8 family). Materiality is one soft shadow on flat pale cards — the ring track is near-identical to its background (today-top.png), card separation on Progress barely registers, and the only textural gesture in the product (the chevron watermark) appears exactly twice, both times inside failure-state voids. Dark mode additionally breaks the settled ~5:1 muted floor ('Fresh start — what's for breakfast?' at ~4.5:1, dark-today.png) and its ring track nearly vanishes.

**Fix:** A token-level pass, not a redesign: (1) nudge the background hue from violet-grey toward genuine warm cream and re-derive the neutral ramp from it; (2) add one warm-tint elevation step used for empty tracks, hero washes, and placeholder grounds — this single token powers the empty-state grammar in move 1; (3) fix the dark-mode muted lightness and track value, and wire both into the existing contrast-audit spec so the class is checked programmatically; (4) decide the texture question once — either the chevron/dot/cross pattern family becomes a deliberate, documented brand texture applied consistently, or it dies everywhere. Because everything is (correctly) tokenised, this is the cheapest move on the list relative to how much it changes what the product photographs like.

#### 7. One copy sweep: kill plumbing vocabulary and unearned claims  `leverage: medium`

**Evidence:** Internal state leaks into user-facing copy on every surface: 'Structured ●' badges repeated on all eight ingredient cards (recipe-detail-desktop.png); 'Open Settings → Connections, enable Apple Health, then pull to refresh' as instruction-manual copy on Today; '0 of 7 days land' jargon; 'PERSONA:' fixture prefixes rendering in production-facing rows (log-sheet.png favourites, today-desktop quick-add); 'Barcode scan is free — always. No paywall. No asterisk.' defensive ad copy on every open of a daily-use sheet; an unlabeled '≈ 20%' with no referent; '0 saves' as anti-social-proof on the app's own recipe; 'Your most consistent week this month' awarded for logging once at 51% under target.

**Fix:** A single editorial pass with three rules, then enforced at review: (1) no pipeline vocabulary — if a state matters to the user, say it in user terms; if it doesn't, silence (confidence badges only appear when confidence is LOW); (2) no unearned claims — superlatives and averages gate on ≥3 data points, save counts suppress below ~10; (3) no self-defence — reassurance copy lives where it converts (onboarding, paywall trust strip under the hero), never as a nag inside a tool. The serif voice promises an editor; the copy needs to have met one.

## Per-surface critiques

### Today dashboard — tier: **functional**

The bones are real — the serif/plum identity, aim-per-slot meal rows, and the desktop ring-with-data are coherent and on-brand — but the below-fold cards give the game away. The "What to eat next" hero is a quarter-screen of muddy olive-grey gradient with a fork glyph where food photography should be, which for a food-first product is the single cheapest pixel in the app. The Activity & energy card renders a giant serif "0 kcal maintenance" directly above a stat row reading "MAINTENANCE 2,117", wrapped in settings-manual copy — a prototype card shipping inside the flagship tab. The fresh-day ring, the emotional centre of the entire product, is a near-invisible grey tick-circle that reads as a stuck loading spinner next to Lifesum's warm gradient wash — and the light palette overall photographs cool lavender-grey, not the warm cream the direction promises. Data-rich states are near-solid; empty states and secondary cards are what pin this at functional.

#### Top issues (ranked by leverage)

- **[major · both] The suggestion hero is a grey placeholder poster, not food**
  - What's wrong: iOS today-mid.png: the 'What to eat next' card spends ~238pt of height (y≈300-1250px @3x) on a muddy olive-to-charcoal gradient, a low-opacity chevron watermark used nowhere else in the app, and an olive fork glyph where a photo should be. Identical on mobile-web (today-scrolled-mobileweb.png). This is the largest card on the surface and it reads as an unloaded image — for a recipe-first brand it is the most expensive-feeling failure on the screen.
  - Fix: Ship real recipe imagery (the AI image-generation project exists for exactly this); until an image resolves, collapse the suggestion to a compact ~72pt row — thumbnail, title, kcal, Log pill — the way Lifesum renders meal suggestions as dense rows, never a full-bleed empty poster. Delete the chevron watermark; it's a one-off texture that violates same-element-same-treatment.
- **[major · both] Fresh-day ring reads as a stuck loading spinner**
  - What's wrong: iOS today-top.png / today-mobileweb.png: at 0 eaten the hero ring is ~66 pale grey-lavender ticks on a near-identical lavender background with a single plum notch at 12 o'clock — visually indistinguishable from an iOS activity indicator. The whole top half of the fresh-day screen is inert: wordmark, date, week strip, then a ghost circle. The emotional anchor of the app has no presence precisely when the user needs a reason to log.
  - Fix: Lifesum puts a warm gradient wash behind the ring so the number reads as a mood even at zero; Cal AI puts a filled 'Log your food' pill directly inside the calorie card, collapsing empty-state friction to one tap. Do both: give the 0% track a visible warm tint one clear step above the background, and put a filled 'Log breakfast' affordance inside the hero on fresh days.
- **[broken · ios] Activity & energy card contradicts its own numbers and leaks plumbing**
  - What's wrong: iOS today-bottom.png: a giant serif '0 kcal maintenance' sits directly above a stat row reading 'MAINTENANCE 2,117' — two different maintenance values on one card. Above it, instruction-manual copy: 'Open Settings → Connections, enable Apple Health, then pull to refresh or revisit this tab to sync.' Three zeros repeat across the card ('0 kcal maintenance', '0 kcal burned', BURNED 0, EATEN 0 — the last duplicating the hero's EATEN 0).
  - Fix: When Health isn't connected, render one compact connect card: icon + one line ('Connect Apple Health to see energy burned') + a single button — and suppress every stat until data exists. Oura never shows a number it can't stand behind; every figure ships with a one-line plain-English interpretation, never sync instructions.
- **[major · web] Desktop copy doesn't track the viewed date: 'Yesterday' over 'Today's Meals'**
  - What's wrong: today-desktop.png / today-scrolled-desktop.png: page heading reads 'Yesterday / Saturday 4 July' (sidebar confirms 'Sun TODAY'), yet the meal ledger below is titled 'Today's Meals', the Planned card says 'Nothing planned for today', and the action reads 'Duplicate day…'. Every label below the heading believes it's today while the heading says yesterday.
  - Fix: Bind all section titles and empty-state copy to the viewed date ('Saturday's meals', 'Nothing was planned for Saturday'). MacroFactor's day view relabels the entire ledger by the selected date — the heading is never the only element that knows what day it is.
- **[major · web] Right rail says the same thing twice, and averages one data point**
  - What's wrong: today-desktop.png: THIS WEEK (bar chart, '929 avg', '1/7 days logged') and DAILY BREAKDOWN (Mon–Sun rows, Sat 929) are the identical dataset rendered as two adjacent cards with two different treatments in the same rail. '929 avg' computed from a single logged day reads as fake math sitting next to the honest '1/7 days logged' caption.
  - Fix: Merge into one weekly card and suppress the average until ≥3 logged days (the 1/7 caption is the honest stat). MacroFactor's swipeable Insights row is the model: one card per signal, each earning its slot — never the same numbers twice.
- **[major · ios] Dark mode breaks the muted-text floor and the ring vanishes**
  - What's wrong: dark-today.png (forensic-confirmed): 'Fresh start — what's for breakfast?' renders at ~4.5:1 lifted violet on near-black — below the settled ~5:1 muted floor — and the ring ticks are barely separable from the background, worse than the already-weak light-mode empty ring. The dark palette otherwise holds (wordmark, week pill, tab bar are fine).
  - Fix: Lift the dark muted-text lightness and cut saturation to clear 5:1, and give the dark ring track a surface value one elevation step above the background. Wire both into the existing contrast-audit spec so the class of bug is checked programmatically, per the root-cause rule.
- **[major · web] Quick-add row clips mid-word with zero affordance**
  - What's wrong: today-desktop.png (forensic-confirmed, already measured in the prior pass — referencing, not re-deriving): third chip 'PERSONA: Chicke..' is amputated flush at the content edge at x≈1065px with no fade mask, no peek geometry, no scroll cue.
  - Fix: 24pt edge gradient fade plus half-card peek so the row visibly continues — Lifesum and MacroFactor horizontal rows always end on a partially visible card, never on a hard clip through a glyph.
- **[minor · both] Ledger chrome inside the emotional hero: BONUS 0 and a bare 'Hide macros' link**
  - What's wrong: All three surfaces: the hero stat row gives a full third of its width to 'BONUS 0' on a fresh day, and 'Hide macros' renders as a naked centred text string floating between hero and macro rings (iOS today-top.png y≈1717px; same on desktop and mobile-web). It reads as debug chrome, not a designed control.
  - Fix: Suppress the BONUS column when zero (or fold it into the GOAL footnote), and move the macros toggle into the card's overflow menu. MacroFactor's one surfaced toggle (Consumed/Remaining) is a designed segmented control under the ring — display preferences it doesn't want in the hero live behind the context menu.

#### Parity gaps

- Suggestion hero dismissal: mobile-web renders a grey-circle X on the 'What to eat next' card (today-scrolled-mobileweb.png, top-right); iOS renders no dismiss at all (today-mid.png) — same card, divergent affordance set, no platform reason.
- Global header furniture: iOS shows bell + avatar (the settings entry); mobile-web shows a recipe-book icon + bell and no avatar — mobile-web loses the settings entry entirely and duplicates the Recipes tab already present in its bottom bar.
- Weekly context layer: desktop carries a streak card ('Day 1 — nice start'), a THIS WEEK bar chart, and a DAILY BREAKDOWN card; iOS Today renders only a text 'WEEKLY INSIGHT' block (today-mid.png) — the week-at-a-glance data visualisation has no iOS equivalent in any capture, a structural gap rather than a data-driven one.
- Fresh-start hero copy differs across surfaces captured within the same rollover window: iOS light says 'Fresh start — log when you're ready.' while iOS dark and mobile-web say 'Fresh start — what's for breakfast?' — if this is date-seeded rotation it's defensible, but as captured it reads as copy drift between platforms.

#### The bar (Mobbin benchmark)

A best-in-class Today dashboard treats the calorie ring as the emotional anchor of the whole screen — large, centred, top-of-fold, with a warm gradient or ambient colour wash behind it (not a flat card) so the number reads as a mood, not a spreadsheet cell. Macros sit immediately below as a lightweight secondary tier (thin bars or small rings, never competing in size with the hero ring), each labeled with plain "X g left" language rather than raw consumed/target math. Below that, the day resolves into a meal-by-meal timeline (breakfast/lunch/dinner/snacks as tappable rows with recommended-calorie ranges or a quick-add affordance) so the ring's abstraction cashes out into concrete next actions. The best examples (Lifesum, MacroFactor) also thread in a secondary signal — a rotating insight, a trend sparkline, or a day-rating nudge — so the screen feels alive and coaching, not just a static ledger.

#### The bar (Mobbin benchmark)

Best-in-class paywalls do three things at once: (1) lead with an emotional full-bleed hero (illustration or photo) that sells the *feeling* of the outcome before any price is shown, (2) collapse the value prop into 3-5 short benefit lines with consistent iconography rather than a dense feature grid, and (3) make the decision nearly frictionless — one visually "winner-tagged" plan (badge like "Best value"/"Most popular"), a single high-contrast CTA, transparent trial-to-charge timeline, and low-anxiety exits (Restore/Terms in small type, never competing with the CTA). The strongest examples (Headspace, Tide) also use a literal timeline component ("Today / In 5 days / In 7 days") to pre-empt the #1 objection — "will I get charged without warning" — which is more persuasive than any comparison table.

- **Headspace** — Warm illustrated full-bleed hero (smiling sun) behind headline, 4 checkmark benefit lines, best-value plan visually distinguished with orange fill + badge, single blue CTA, Restore/Terms demoted to small link row below the fold of the CTA — nothing competes with the buy action ([Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/screens/34b91e79-84d9-48c5-a3bd-6cce3a4ef4e3))
- **Tide** — Trust badges (App Store award, star rating) placed directly under the hero headline before any pricing — borrows credibility ahead of the ask; explicit 3-step trial timeline card (Today / In 5 days / In 7 days) that answers the 'will I be surprise-charged' objection before it's asked ([Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/screens/4946c946-896e-433d-a459-e031e0e011b6))
- **Calm Sleep** — Benefit lines are outcome-framed sentences ('Fall asleep faster with...') not feature nouns, each separated by hairline dividers for scannability; annual plan gets a blue-bordered selected state with strikethrough original price to dramatize the discount math inline rather than in a separate table ([Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/screens/07905baf-c7e7-48db-9b78-5866d040e1af))
- **Beside** — Dark, premium editorial tone (not candy-colored) with a glowing radial hero treatment; free-trial toggle is a distinct dark card with its own checkmark state, separated from the plan-selection cards below — decouples 'do I trust the trial terms' from 'which plan do I pick' ([Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/screens/aacc9503-be4e-481d-b6e4-df0dba561324))
- **Synthesia** — Desktop/web comparison-table pattern: sticky plan-column headers with CTA buttons pinned at the top of the table (not just the bottom), one column visually highlighted (tinted background) as the recommended tier, grouped feature rows under bold section labels so a long feature list stays scannable ([Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/screens/28175899-b402-4c29-970f-18234411d48c))
