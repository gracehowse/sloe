# Suppr Brand Guidelines

Single reference for voice, tone, messaging, visual direction, and naming conventions across web and mobile.

---

## 1. Mission

Help people eat well without making it their whole personality. Suppr gives you the tools to understand what you eat, plan what you cook, and track progress toward your goals — then gets out of the way.

## 2. Values

| Value | What it means in practice |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Clarity over cleverness** | Every number, label, and CTA should be immediately understandable. No jargon, no ambiguity. |
| **Honesty over hype** | Nutrition data is estimated, not gospel. We say so. We never promise outcomes. |
| **Autonomy over authority** | We surface data and options; the user decides. No guilt, no streaks-as-punishment, no "you failed" language. |
| **Craft over clutter** | Every screen earns its place. Premium means restraint, not decoration. |

## 3. Target Audience

**Primary:** Health-conscious adults (25-45) who cook regularly, care about what they eat, and want accurate nutrition data without the noise of traditional calorie counters.

**Not:** Competitive bodybuilders, clinical dietitians, people seeking medical nutrition therapy, or teenagers.

**Psychographic:** They read ingredient labels. They have saved recipes on Instagram they never went back to. They have tried MyFitnessPal and found it tedious or inaccurate. They want something that respects their intelligence.

## 4. Brand Positioning

**Category:** Recipe and nutrition tracking platform.

**Positioning statement:** Suppr is the recipe and nutrition platform for people who actually cook. Unlike calorie counters that treat food as numbers, Suppr starts with real recipes from the sites you already use, breaks down the macros automatically, and helps you plan meals that hit your targets.

**Competitive distinction (internal reference only — never name competitors in user-facing copy):**

| Competitor | Their angle | Suppr's counter |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| MyFitnessPal | Massive food database, social features | Suppr starts from recipes, not barcodes. Nutrition is verified against USDA, not user-submitted. |
| Lose It! | Gamified weight loss, streaks | Suppr respects your autonomy. No guilt mechanics, no "you went over" shame. |
| MacroFactor | Algorithm-driven, data-heavy | Suppr is approachable. You don't need to understand TDEE math to use it well. |
| Cronometer | Clinical precision, micronutrients | Suppr is built around cooking, not lab reports. |
| Yazio | Meal plans, fasting | Suppr's recipes come from real food creators, not generic stock photography. |

**Rule:** Never name or disparage competitors in user-facing copy, marketing, or App Store descriptions. Let features speak for themselves. Say what Suppr does — not what others don't.

## 5. Brand Personality

Suppr is **knowledgeable but never preachy**, **precise but never clinical**, **confident but never loud**.

Think: a friend who happens to be a great cook and knows their nutrition science. They'll tell you the facts, give you good options, and never make you feel bad about a pizza.

**Five personality traits:**

1. **Grounded** — We deal in real food, real data, real cooking. No superfood nonsense.
2. **Precise** — We care about accuracy. Confidence scores exist for a reason.
3. **Warm** — We celebrate good meals, not perfect macros.
4. **Understated** — Premium means quiet confidence, not shouting.
5. **Respectful** — Your body, your goals, your pace. We never prescribe.

## 6. Tone of Voice

### Register

Conversational but competent. The tone sits between "casual app" and "professional tool" — closer to a well-designed fintech than a fitness app.

### Rules

| Rule | Example (good) | Example (bad) |
|------|----------------|---------------|
| **Use plain language** | "Your daily calorie target" | "Your personalised nutritional energy allocation" |
| **Be specific, not vague** | "Matched against USDA FoodData Central" | "Powered by science" |
| **State facts, not commands** | "You've logged 1,420 of 1,800 calories" | "You need to eat 380 more calories!" |
| **Qualify health claims** | "Based on the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, your estimated TDEE is..." | "Your TDEE is 2,100 calories" |
| **Use active voice** | "Suppr parses every ingredient" | "Every ingredient is parsed" |
| **Avoid diet culture language** | "Create a calorie deficit" | "Blast fat" / "Shed pounds" / "Guilt-free" |
| **Never shame** | "380 calories remaining" | "You went over by 200 calories!" |
| **Never be sycophantic** | "Your targets are set" | "Amazing job setting up your profile!" |

### Hedging language for nutrition

Always use when displaying calculated or estimated values:

- "estimated" (TDEE, projections)
- "based on" (methodology references)
- "approximately" (weight projections)
- "matched against" (database lookups)

Never use:

- "exactly" / "precisely" (for nutrition values)
- "guaranteed" / "proven" (for outcomes)
- "you should" / "you need to" (prescriptive advice)
- "just" (minimising — "just log your meals")

### Tense rules

| Context | Tense | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Live data (today's tracker) | Present | "380 remaining" / "1,420 logged" |
| Projected values (today, future) | Present with qualifier | "Projected total: 2,340" / "Estimated remaining: 580" |
| Completed days (yesterday, historical) | Past | "Final burn: 2,180" / "Bonus earned: +120" |
| Goal projections | Future with qualifier | "You could reach 60 kg by approximately 14 July" |
| Onboarding / setup | Present | "Your targets are set" |
| Instructions / help | Present imperative | "Tap any ingredient to see its confidence score" |

### Projection language by screen

Different screens show projections differently. Each must be distinct and unambiguous:

| Screen | Label | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Burn detail (today) | "Projected total" | Combines actual + estimated remaining burn |
| Burn detail (past) | "Final burn" | Completed, no estimation |
| Weight tracker | "Projected weight" | Based on intake trend, always qualified |
| Onboarding projection | "You could reach [weight] by [date]" | Aspirational, uses "could" not "will" |
| Activity bonus (today) | "Bonus so far" | Incomplete day, will change |
| Activity bonus (past) | "Bonus earned" | Finalised |
| Calorie ring (today) | "[remaining] left" | Simple, present tense |

## 7. Messaging Hierarchy

### Elevator pitch (10 seconds)

> Suppr is a recipe and nutrition platform. Import recipes from anywhere, get verified macros, plan meals that hit your targets.

### Tagline options

- **"Recipes, macros & meal planning"** (current — functional, accurate, keep as primary)
- **"How you actually eat"** (positioning — for marketing contexts where the full tagline is too long)

Do not use:

- "Eat smarter" (too close to Lose It!)
- "Track everything" (too close to MyFitnessPal)
- "Fuel your body" (fitness culture, not us)
- "The future of food tracking" (meaningless)

### Key messages (for marketing pages, App Store, PR)

1. **Recipe-first tracking.** Import from Instagram, TikTok, or any recipe site. Suppr parses every ingredient and matches it against USDA, Open Food Facts, and FatSecret.
2. **Nutrition you can trust.** Every ingredient gets a confidence score. You always see the source and can verify or edit any value.
3. **Plans that fit your life.** Meal plans built from your saved recipes, optimised for your macro targets. Shopping lists generated automatically.
4. **Apple Health integration.** Activity-adjusted calories that account for what you actually burn, without double-counting your baseline.

### CTA copy

| Context | CTA | Do not use |
|---------|-----|-----------|
| Free tier signup | "Get Started" | "Sign Up Free" / "Join Now" |
| Upgrade to Base | "Upgrade to Base" | "Go Pro" / "Unlock Everything" |
| Upgrade to Pro | "Upgrade to Pro" | "Start Your Journey" |
| Trial start | "Start Free Trial" | "Try It Free" / "Claim Your Trial" |
| Continue without paying | "Continue for free" | "No thanks" / "Maybe later" / "Skip" (as a CTA label — "Skip" is acceptable as a navigation action during onboarding) |
| Onboarding completion | "Finish setup" (web) / "Get Started" (mobile) | "Let's go!" / "You're all set!" (as a button label) |
| Save action | "Save" | "Save Changes" (unless disambiguating from another save) |
| Import recipe | "Import" | "Add Recipe" (we import, not add) |

## 8. Naming Conventions

### Feature names

Use plain English. Do not brand individual features.

| Feature | Name | Do not call it |
|---------|------|---------------|
| Calorie/macro tracker | "Tracker" / "Daily tracker" | "Dashboard" / "MyDay" / "FoodLog" |
| Recipe library | "Library" or "Saved recipes" | "Cookbook" / "Recipe Box" / "Vault" |
| Recipe discovery | "Discover" | "Explore" / "Feed" / "Browse" |
| Meal planner | "Meal plan" / "Planner" | "Schedule" / "Calendar" (it's not a generic calendar) |
| Barcode scanning | "Scan" / "Barcode scan" | "Scanner" / "SnapScan" |
| Photo logging | "Photo (AI)" | "Snap to Track" / "FoodCam" |
| Voice logging | "Voice" | "Voice AI" / "SpeakTrack" |
| Import from URL | "Recipe import" | "URL grab" / "Web scrape" |
| Nutrition confidence | "Confidence score" | "Trust score" / "Accuracy rating" |
| Activity bonus | "Activity bonus" | "Earn back" / "Exercise calories" |
| Weight projection | "Weight projection" | "Goal tracker" / "Progress prediction" |

### Tier names

- **Free** — always capitalised, no descriptor needed
- **Base** — the mid-tier, not "Basic" or "Standard"
- **Pro** — the top tier, not "Premium" or "Ultimate"

### Product name

- **Suppr** — always capitalised, never "SUPPR" or "suppr"
- **Suppr Pro** — when referring to the Pro subscription specifically
- In running text, do not italicise or bold the product name unless it's the first mention in a marketing context

## 9. Visual Direction

### Colour

Defined in `docs/ux/brand-tokens.md`. The canonical palette lives in `apps/mobile/constants/theme.ts` (mobile) and `src/styles/theme.css` (web). These must stay aligned.

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Primary accent | `#4c6ce0` (blue) | Buttons, selected states, links, brand wordmark |
| Primary light | `#6c8cff` | Dark mode tint, active tab (dark), protein macro |
| Marketing gradient | `#4c6ce0` to `#e04888` | Hero sections, paywall headers, onboarding emphasis only |
| Success | `#22a860` | Confirmations, positive states, calorie tracking |
| Warning | `#e8a020` | Caution states, over-budget indicator, activity bonus |
| Destructive | `#e04848` | Errors, dangerous actions only |
| Magenta | `#e04888` | Fat macro, gradient accent — not for body UI |
| Cyan | `#06b6d4` | Water tracking, exercise/activity |
| Orange | `#f97316` | Sodium macro |

**Macro colours (fixed across all screens):**

| Macro | Hex | Token |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Calories | `#22a860` (success) | `MacroColors.calories` |
| Protein | `#4c6ce0` (primary) | `MacroColors.protein` |
| Carbs | `#e8a020` (warning) | `MacroColors.carbs` |
| Fat | `#e04888` (magenta) | `MacroColors.fat` |
| Fiber | `#22a860` (success) | `MacroColors.fiber` |
| Water | `#06b6d4` (cyan) | `MacroColors.water` |

**Rules:**
- Magenta is never used as a standalone accent in the product UI — only in gradients and the fat macro.
- One primary accent per screen region. Do not use primary and primaryLight as competing accents on the same surface.
- Macro colours are fixed via `MacroColors` and must not change per-screen. Never hardcode hex values — always reference theme tokens.
- No hardcoded hex values in component files. All colours must come from theme tokens or `useThemeColors()`.
- **Over-budget states use warning (amber), never destructive (red).** Red implies failure. Amber is adherence-neutral. Transition: 0–100% of goal = success, 100–120% = warning, 120%+ = deep warning. Never use the word "over" — use "above target" or simply show the number.

**Why blue, not violet:** Suppr's primary shifted from violet (#7c3aed) to blue (#4c6ce0) during the design overhaul. Blue reads as precise and trustworthy without being clinical. While violet is unclaimed in the nutrition category, the blue primary harmonises better with the macro colour system and avoids the "lifestyle brand" feel that violet can carry. The violet heritage is preserved in the `Neon` legacy alias and marketing gradients.

### Typography

- **Web:** Inter via `next/font`. Headings are `font-bold` (700) or `font-semibold` (600), never `font-black` in body UI.
- **Mobile:** System font (San Francisco on iOS). Semantic weight scale:

| Weight | Token | Usage |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 400 | regular | Body text, descriptions |
| 500 | medium | Subtle emphasis (rarely used) |
| 600 | semibold | Labels, section titles within cards |
| 700 | bold | Screen titles, headings, CTAs |
| 800 | heavy | Hero numbers (calorie totals, stat grid, fasting timer) |

Do not use `fontWeight: "900"`. Maximum weight in the product UI is 800.

- **Numbers:** Always use `tabular-nums` / `fontVariant: ["tabular-nums"]` for any numerical display that may change (calories, weights, counters). This prevents layout shift.
- **Letter spacing:** Tighten to `-0.02em` on headings. Tighten further to `-0.03em` on display numbers (36px+). Use `0.1em` tracking on uppercase overline labels (e.g., "WEEKLY INSIGHT", "SOURCE").

### Layout

- **Border radius:** Use tokens exclusively. `Radius.lg` (16px) for cards, `Radius.md` (12px) for inputs and buttons, `Radius.sm` (8px) for chips and badges, `Radius.full` for pills. Never use raw `borderRadius: 14` — pick the nearest token.
- **Spacing:** Use `Spacing` tokens on mobile. Never use raw pixel values like `paddingVertical: 14` — use `Spacing.lg` (16) or `Spacing.md` (12). If the gap between tokens is too large, add the value to the token set rather than hardcoding.
- **Cards:** Defined by border + subtle shadow. Light: `border-slate-200`, `shadow-sm`. Dark: `border` from theme, no visible shadow. No heavy drop shadows.
- **Dark mode:** Dark mode is the primary design context — design for dark first, adapt for light. Dark backgrounds use `#0a0a0f` (mobile) / `#101014` (web). Never use pure black (`#000`). Dark mode text should be `#e4e4e8` range, never pure white (`#ffffff`) — pure white causes halation on OLED.
- **Surface hierarchy:** Level 0 = page background, Level 1 = cards, Level 2 = modals/popovers. Use `color + "08"` for subtle surface tints (standardise to one opacity, not a mix of "08", "12", "18", "20").

### Imagery & Iconography

- **Icons:** **Lucide on both platforms** (`lucide-react` web, `lucide-react-native` mobile). Decided 2026-04-28 — the prior "Lucide web / Ionicons mobile" rule disagreed with the code (~31 mobile files used Lucide including the tab bar nav and meal slot icons; ~64 used Ionicons; agents kept "fixing" the same Sparkles import in different directions). Lucide has the sharper vocabulary for nutrition concepts (Flame, Footprints, ChefHat, Beef, Wheat, etc.) and aligns mobile with web. Prefer outline variants for navigation and in-card icons; filled for active/selected tab states. Existing Ionicons usages migrate opportunistically — no rush, but new code uses Lucide. Reference: `docs/ux/teardown-2026-04-28-daily-loop.md` Top-5 #4.
- **No stock photography** in the product UI. Recipe images come from imported sources.
- **No illustrations or mascots.** The product's visual identity comes from its typography, spacing, and colour — not decorative elements.
- **Empty states:** Use an `IconBox` with a themed icon — never use emoji as illustration (no shopping cart emoji, no sparkle emoji). Match the quality of the Progress empty state everywhere.
- **Gradients:** Primary-to-magenta (`#4c6ce0` to `#e04888`) for marketing CTAs and hero sections. Do not use gradients in the core product UI — flat colour only.

## 10. Copy Examples

### Good copy (use as reference)

**Pricing page hero:**
> "Everything you need to eat well"

Why it works: Confident but not hyperbolic. "Eat well" is our territory — not "lose weight" or "get shredded."

**Tier description (Base):**
> "The full meal planning loop"

Why it works: Specific to what the tier unlocks. "Loop" implies a workflow (import > plan > shop > cook), not just a feature list.

**Help page methodology:**
> "When you import a recipe, Suppr parses every ingredient and matches it against multiple food databases."

Why it works: Active voice, specific, tells you what happens without overselling.

**Onboarding (mobile):**
> "We'll tailor your calorie and macro targets to match."

Why it works: States the benefit of the step you just completed. Not "Great choice!" or "You're doing amazing!"

**Burn detail (today):**
> "Based on your resting rate so far today"

Why it works: Qualifies the estimate, references the source, uses present tense for a live value.

**Burn detail (past):**
> "Any burn above your maintenance estimate was added to your food budget for this day."

Why it works: Past tense for a completed day. "Estimate" hedges the maintenance figure. Clear cause-and-effect.

**Budget confirmation (mobile):**
> "Suppr will use this to find recipes and build meal plans that fit your target. You can adjust it anytime in settings."

Why it works: Explains what happens next, gives the user control, no exclamation marks.

### Bad copy (do not write like this)

| Bad | Why | Better |
|-----|-----|--------|
| "Congratulations on taking the first step!" | Patronising. They signed up, they didn't summit Everest. | "Your targets are set." |
| "You only have 200 calories left — choose wisely!" | Shame-adjacent, prescriptive. | "200 remaining" |
| "Unlock your full potential" | Generic, could be any app. | "Unlimited saved recipes" (say what they get) |
| "Our AI-powered engine analyses your nutrition" | Buzzword soup. Which AI? What does it do? | "Suppr matches each ingredient against USDA FoodData Central" |
| "Start your weight loss journey today" | Diet culture, competitor territory. | "Import your first recipe" (action-oriented) |
| "Smart meal plan generation" | "Smart" is meaningless. Everything claims to be smart. | "Meal plans built from your saved recipes" |
| "Track smarter, not harder" | Cliche. Also implies the user has been doing it wrong. | Remove entirely. |
| "Your TDEE is 2,100 calories" | Unqualified health claim. | "Your estimated TDEE is approximately 2,100 calories (Mifflin-St Jeor)" |
| "You failed to meet your protein target" | Shame. We never use "fail." | "Protein: 82g of 130g" (just state the numbers) |

## 11. Dark Patterns We Will Never Use

These patterns are common in the nutrition app category. Suppr explicitly prohibits all of them.

| Pattern | Used by | Why we reject it |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Countdown timers on paywall | Yazio | Creates false urgency. Dishonest. |
| Weekly pricing to obscure true cost | Yazio | Deceptive — $6.99/week sounds cheap, $363/year does not. |
| "Limited time offer" urgency | Yazio, MFP | Artificial scarcity on a digital product. |
| Interstitial upsells during core flows | MFP | Interrupting meal logging to sell Premium is hostile. |
| "If every day were like today, you'd weigh X" | MFP, Lose It! | Unqualified health claim disguised as motivation. |
| Streaks as punishment | MFP, Lose It! | Breaking a streak should not feel like failure. |
| "You went over" / "You failed" shame language | MFP, Lose It! | Adherence-neutral design means no editorial judgment on numbers. |
| Red colour for over-budget calories | Most competitors | Red = failure. We use amber (adherence-neutral). |
| Fake personalisation ("Your plan is ready!") | Yazio, Noom | Implying a "plan" was created when it's the same for everyone. |
| Requiring account before showing value | MFP, Yazio | Let users see the product before asking them to sign up. |
| Hiding the "Continue free" option | Multiple | The free option must be equally prominent to the paid CTA. |
| Gamified leaderboards / social comparison | Noom, MFP | Comparing your nutrition to others is harmful. |

## 12. Disclaimers & Legal Copy

Every surface that shows calculated nutrition, projections, or health-adjacent data must include appropriate hedging. The full disclaimer set lives on `/help` but key principles apply everywhere:

- **Nutrition values:** "Values are estimates. Actual values vary by preparation method, brand, and portion size."
- **Weight projections:** Always use "could" or "approximately." Never "will" or "guaranteed."
- **Calorie targets:** Reference the calculation method (Mifflin-St Jeor) on any screen that shows how the target was derived.
- **Medical:** "Suppr is a personal tracking tool, not a medical device. Consult a healthcare professional before making significant dietary changes."
- **AI features:** "Results are estimates and should be reviewed before saving."
- **Minimum intake:** The app does not enforce minimums, but the onboarding displays a warning for very low calorie plans: "Very low calorie — consult a doctor before starting."

## 13. What Suppr Sounds Like vs. Does Not Sound Like

| Suppr sounds like | Suppr does not sound like |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| A knowledgeable friend who cooks | A personal trainer yelling at you |
| A well-designed tool that explains itself | A clinical report from a lab |
| Calm confidence | Breathless startup enthusiasm |
| "Here's what the data says" | "You NEED to try this!" |
| "380 remaining" | "Only 380 left, keep going!" |
| "Estimated TDEE based on Mifflin-St Jeor" | "Our proprietary algorithm" |
| "Import recipes from anywhere" | "AI-powered recipe intelligence engine" |
| "You can adjust this anytime" | "Don't worry, we've got you covered!" |
