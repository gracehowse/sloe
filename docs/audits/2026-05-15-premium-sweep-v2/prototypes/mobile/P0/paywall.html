What changed

  • #13.1 SUBTRACT Remove the "Subscriptions unavailable" card from the default render path. What it duplicates / weakens: the trust-chip strip below already carries "Cancel anytime / refund / price locked" — which is the inverse of "unavailable" trust. Two competing trust posters in the same viewport is worse than one. The unavailable case now degrades to a 1-line footnote beneath the price ("If pricing doesn't load, pull to refresh") — only rendered when RC offerings genuinely fail to resolve.
  • #13.2 TIGHTEN Move trust chips inline adjacent to the price, inside the Pro card (allowed borrow from Stripe Checkout per DC4: "adjacent placement of guarantees and price"). Chips downsize from 13px to 11px and lose the soft green background; the trust signal compresses but stays readable. What it duplicates / weakens: nothing — the original block layout above the toggle is removed and the chips relocate to where Stripe puts them. Reduces vertical chrome by ~80px.

DC4 preservation check

The three explicit guarantees (Cancel anytime · 7-day refund · Price locked) are preserved verbatim — the bet (specificity, not vibes) is intact. Placement changes from "above the price block" to "inside the Pro card adjacent to the price digit", which is the Stripe Checkout borrow explicitly listed as allowed in defended-choices.md §DC4. No "No price hikes ever" 4th chip added (deferred pending Grace's long-term commitment per the DC reference doc).

Refused borrows considered

Considered adopting Calm's full "Not satisfied? Cancel anytime, no questions asked" longer-form guarantee copy in place of the short chips. Refused — Suppr's chip-form specificity ("Cancel anytime in App Store") is more falsifiable than Calm's vibes-style promise, and DC4 explicitly defends the chip form against "generic 'Trusted by N users' social proof" patterns.

Capture gaps still open

Dark-mode paywall pixel is missing (RTP-7) — this prototype renders the after-state in light only. The dark variant needs a re-capture via 00z_premium_bar_dark.yaml before G4 (per-item sim validation) can sign off on this item. The DC4 trust chips will need contrast verification on near-black canvas — the soft green chip background may not survive dark mode at AA contrast.
